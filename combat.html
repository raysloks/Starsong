Basics

Strike

Roll your weapon's damage dice. The result is capped to your Strength. Deal the final result as damage to your target.

Shot

Roll your weapon's damage dice. The result is capped to the weapon's damage limit. Deal the final result as damage to your target.

Movement

Most movement in combat occurs through the actions Charge, Stride, and Dash.

Actions

On your turn you may take one action.

Note that the actions listed here are not an exhaustive list.

Melee Attack

Action

Make a Strike against an adjacent target.

You may move a number of paces before and after striking, totalling less than or equal to your Speed.

However, the Strike gains a damage penalty equal to the number of paces moved. This penalty is applied before the damage is capped.

Punishable by unmarked targets you move away from.

Ranged Attack

Action

Make a Shot against a target in range.

You may move a number of paces before and after shooting, totalling less than or equal to your Speed.

However, the Shot gains a damage penalty equal to the number of paces moved. This penalty is applied before the damage is capped.

Punishable by unmarked targets you move away from.

Note that you do not automatically mark adjacent enemies while using a ranged weapon.

Stride

Action

Move a number of paces equal to or less than your Speed.

Punishable by unmarked targets you move away from.

Dash

Action

Move a number of paces equal to or less than three times your Speed plus 1d6.

You cannot mark targets whilst dashing.

Punishable by any targets.

Charge

Action

Move a number of paces equal to or less than your Speed in a straight line towards a target and make a Strike against it.

You must move at least 4 paces.

Hold

Action

Select a combatant that has not yet taken their turn this round.

Your turn is put on hold until after they end their turn.

Quaff

Action

Drink a potion.

Punishable by any targets.

Wait

Action

Do nothing.

Reactions

React

Reaction

Make an Agility-save.

Punish

Free Reaction

When an adjacent enemy performs a Punishable action, you may Punish them.

Make a Strike against the target.

Damage

Wounds

If a hit inflicts damage, it may also inflict a wound. A hit can only inflict a single wound, and will inflict the most severe type of wound it is able to.

Below is a list of conditions that cause a hit to inflict a wound.

ConditionWound
Damage equal to or larger than half the target's current hit points.Minor
Damage equal to or larger than the target's current hit points.Major
Damage equal to or larger than twice the target's current hit points.Fatal

Minor wounds will never incur permanent penalties nor cause death.

Major wounds may incur permanent penalties if not treated well, and may cause death if treated poorly or not at all.

Fatal wounds will always incur permanent penalties, and will almost always cause death.

Tables can be found here.

Nonlethal

Nonlethal damage can never inflict fatal wounds and always uses the unarmed wounds table.

Conditions

Stunned

May not take any actions or reactions.

Strikes against stunned targets have advantage.

Prone

Movement limited to one pace.

Agility-saves are {% include unlucky.html %}.

Strikes and Shots are impaired, unless the weapon has the 'prone' tag.

Strikes against prone targets are enhanced.

Shots against prone targets are impaired, unless there is a large height advantage.
