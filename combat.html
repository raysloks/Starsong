Basics

Strike

Roll your weapon's damage dice, exploding. The result is capped to your Strength. Deal the final result as damage to your target.

If a Strike has advantage, you may reroll each damage die once. If a Strike has disadvantage, the damage dice do not explode.

Shot

Roll your weapon's damage dice, exploding. The result is capped to the weapon's damage limit. Deal the final result as damage to your target.

If a Shot has advantage, you may reroll each damage die once. If a Shot has disadvantage, the damage dice do not explode.

Movement

Most movement in combat occurs through the actions Charge, Stride, and Dash.

Turns

At the start of a time-critical encounter, every player rolls a d20 to use as their Initiative. This value may be modified by various abilities, but it is always a value between 1 and 20.

Regardless, record the result as your Initiative. You could do this via sticky notes or some sort of dry-erase stuff.

The players begin taking turns, starting at the highest Initiative.

Actions

On your turn you may take one action.

Note that the actions listed here are not an exhaustive list.

Melee Attack

Action

Make a Strike against an adjacent target.

Ranged Attack

Action

Allows Opportunity Attacks.

Make a Shot against a target in range.

Stride

Action

Move a number of paces equal to or less than your Speed.

Dash

Action

Move a number of paces equal to or less than your Speed plus 1d6.

Allows Opportunity Attacks from anyone you move away from or pass on the way.

Charge

Action

Move up to 5 paces towards a target and make a Strike against it.

Allows Opportunity Attacks from anyone you move away from or pass on the way.

Recover

Action

Regain a number of hit points equal to your Constitution, and reduce your maximum hit points by 1.

Lost maximum hit points can be recovered by resting.

Hold

Action

Put your turn on hold. After any other creature's action you may interject, using your reaction to perform an action.

Holding your turn does not change your Initiative.

Wait

Action

Reactions

Dodge

When you are the target of a Strike or Shot, you may attempt to Dodge the attack.

Parry

When you are the target of a Strike, you may attempt to Parry the attack.

Punish

When an adjacent enemy performs a Punishable action, you may Punish them.

Make a Strike against the target.

Damage

Wounds

If a hit inflicts damage, it may also inflict a wound. A hit can only inflict a single wound, and will inflict the most severe type of wound it is able to.

Below is a list of conditions that cause a hit to inflict a wound.

ConditionWound
Damage equal to or larger than the target's Constitution.Minor
Damage equal to or larger than the target's current hit points.Major
Damage equal to or larger than the target's current hit points plus the target's Constitution.Fatal

Minor wounds will never incur permanent penalties.

Major wounds may incur permanent penalties if not treated well, and may be fatal if treated poorly or not at all.

Fatal wounds are very often fatal, and will in most cases incur permanent penalties should death not occur.

Tables can be found here.

Nonlethal

Nonlethal damage can never inflict fatal wounds and always uses the unarmed wounds table.

Conditions

Stunned

May not take any actions or reactions.

Strikes against stunned targets have advantage.

Prone

Movement limited to one pace.

Disadvantage on Dexterity tests.

Disadvantage on Strikes and Shots, unless the weapon has the 'prone' tag.

Strikes against prone targets have advantage.

Shots against prone targets have disadvantage.

May use an action to stand up, moving up to one pace and allowing Opportunity Attacks.
