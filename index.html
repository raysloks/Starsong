Preface

These rules are very much a work in progress, they are not ready for actual play. If you have any suggestions or comments, please let me know.

Goals

  • Keep combat somewhat fast and smooth.
  • Keep total hit points somewhat low.
  • Loss of hit points should be felt, but there should be no need to whittle down the players resources with multiple encounters to provide challenge.

Character Stats

While a character is so much more than just stats, they provide a good baseline.

For creating a character, see here.

Primary

Strength

The maximum damage you can deal with a single melee attack.

Agility

How many paces you can move in a single turn.

How much damage you reduce with the Dodge reaction.

Intelligence

Logic, reasoning, and remembering random facts.

Constitution

Increases you maximum hit points.

Dexterity

Parrying.

Precise fingerwork.

Wisdom

Instincts, common sense, and willpower.

Charisma

Social skills, controlling how others see you.

Secondary

These stats are derived from your primary stats, your perks, and your equipment.

Hit Points

Your base hit point total is equal to:

Constitution + Level

Armor

Every point of Armor reduces the raw damage you take by 1.

Skills

When you allocate a skill point, you must first roll a d6. You can then add the point to any skill in which your current rank is lower than the result.

If you have enough ranks in a skill, you may make relevant checks without disadvantage.

Additionally, each rank grants a +1 bonus.

Gameplay

Rolling Dice

Tests

Roll a d20 and add any relevant modifiers.

A test may use a stat; a skill; both; or neither as modifiers.

If the result is larger than or equal to the check's Difficulty Class, you succeed at the test.

An average check has a DC of 16.

Tests are sometimes referred to as checks or saves.

Why does an average check have a DC of 16? I should have recorded my reasoning.

Contests

Roll a d20 and add any relevant modifiers.

Your opponent does the same.

Whoever has the largest result wins.

If both results are the same, nothing happens most of the time.

Examples

Leveling Up

When leveling up, you perform the following:

  • Increase your base hit points by 1 (included in the base hit points calculation above).
  • Advance a career or start a new career.
  • Gain one skill rank.
  • Attempt to improve one primary stat.

Careers

Skill Ranks

Allocate a skill point as you did during character creation.

Note that you will always be able to improve one skill.

Primary Stats

Select one primary stat you would like to attempt to improve. Roll a d20. If the result is higher than your current stat, you may increase the stat by 1.

Note that you must select which stat you aim to improve before rolling the die.
